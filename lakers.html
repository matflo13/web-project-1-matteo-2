1971-72 Los Angeles Lakers

The 1971-72 Los Angeles Lakers are widely regarded as one of the greatest basketball teams of all time. Led by Hall of Famers Jerry West and Wilt Chamberlain, the Lakers finished the regular season with a record of 69-13, the best in NBA history at the time. This season stood alone as the best regular season record for the next 24 years, when the 1995-96 Chicago Bulls went 72-10. They went on to win the NBA Championship, defeating the New York Knicks in the Finals 4-1.

The Lakers Star Trio

The 1971-72 Los Angeles Lakers were led by a trio of superstars: Jerry West, Wilt Chamberlain, and Gail Goodrich. At the beginning of the season, they also had all-time great Elgin Baylor, but he retired 9 games into the season after realizing his body could not take another season. Still, this group of stars thrived in the league, recording a 33-game win streak, and leading the Lakers to a 69-13 record and an NBA championship.

Jerry West

Jerry West, also known as "Mr. Clutch," was the Lakers' leader and primary scorer. West was known for his insanely accurate shooting and his ability to perform in clutch moments. He was a 14-time NBA All-Star and was named to the All-NBA First Team 12 times during his career. West was also a great defender, earning 5 All-Defensive Team selections over his career. After his retirement, West was made the logo of the NBA and became a successful executive who was a key factor in building the Lakers' championship teams in the 1980s.

Wilt Chamberlain

Wilt Chamberlain was one of the most dominant players in NBA history. Standing at 7'1", he was a physical force on the court, known for his unstoppable scoring ability and rebounding skill. Wilt averaged 50.4 points per game during the 1961-62 season, a record that still stands today. He also averaged an incredible 30.1 points and 22.9 rebounds per game over his career, and he holds many NBA records, including scoring 100 points in a single game. However, in the later years of his career when his athleticism declined, Wilt focused more on defense, rebounding and team-oriented basketball. In the 1971-72 season, he averaged a career-low 14.8 points per game, but he was a key rebounder for the Lakers, leading the league in boards. He was a 13-time All-Star and won 4 MVP awards during his career, including one MVP award in his rookie year. Wilt won his second NBA championship with the Lakers in 1972.

Gail Goodrich

Gail Goodrich was a key player for the Lakers during their championship run in the early 1970s. Known for his smooth scoring ability, Goodrich was a 5-time All-Star and was named to the All-NBA First Team in 1974. He played a crucial role in the Lakers' success, providing reliable scoring alongside Jerry West and Wilt Chamberlain.

Conclusion

The 1971-72 Los Angeles Lakers were a dominant force in the NBA, led by Jerry West, Wilt Chamberlain, and Gail Goodrich, a near-unstoppable trio. This team reshaped the league with their record-breaking 69-13 regular season and their NBA championship victory, setting a high bar for other great teams to meet. They are remembered as one of the best teams in NBA history and one of the 1st "superteams" in the league.
