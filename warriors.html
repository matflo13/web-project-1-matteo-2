2016-17 Golden State Warriors

The mid to late 2010s were dominated the Golden State Warriors dynasty, and the 2016-17 season was the pinnacle of their success. The 2016-17 Golden State Warriors are widely regarded as one of the greatest basketball teams of all time. Coming off a historic 73-9 regular season in 2015-16, the Warriors were determined to defend their 2015 championship. However, after claiming a commanding 3-1 lead over the Cleveland Cavaliers, they ultimately lost the next 3 games straight, losing the series. After the historic Finals disappointment, the Warriors bounced back with a vengeance. They returned with the same trio of future Hall of Famers Stephen Curry, Klay Thompson, and Draymond Green, and even added Kevin Durant, an all-time great scorer and future Hall of Famer. The Warriors finished the regular season with a record of 67-15, securing the top seed in the Western Conference. They went on to dominate the postseason, sweeping the Portland Trail Blazers, Utah Jazz, and San Antonio Spurs on the way to the NBA Finals. In the Finals, they faced the Cleveland Cavaliers for the third consecutive year, and this time they were determined to claim the championship. The Warriors won the series in five games, with Kevin Durant being named Finals MVP for his outstanding performance throughout the series. The 2016-17 Golden State Warriors will be remembered for their incredible talent, teamwork, and dominance on both ends of the floor, solidifying their place in NBA history as one of the greatest teams of all time.

Key Players

The 2016-17 Golden State Warriors were led by a core group of superstars: Stephen Curry, Kevin Durant, Klay Thompson, and Draymond Green. This group was a massive part of the team's success, with each of them bringing their unique skills and talents to the court.

Stephen Curry

Stephen Curry is widely regarded as the greatest shooter in NBA history. His ability to make shots from long range has changed the way the entire game is played, and he is a two-time NBA MVP. Curry's incredible ball-handling skills and quick release make him a constant threat on the court.

Kevin Durant

Kevin Durant is one of the most versatile scorers in the history of the NBA. He has the ability to shoot over defenders and is also an excellent ball-handler. He is listed as 6'10, but his real height is speculated to be anywhere from 6'9 to 7'0. Durant was named the Finals MVP in 2017 and 2018, solidifying his status as one of the game's greats.

Klay Thompson

Klay Thompson is known for his incredible shooting ability and lockdown defense. As one half of the "Splash Brothers" duo with Curry, Thompson has been a key contributor to the Warriors' success. He is also a five-time NBA All-Star.

Draymond Green

Draymond Green is the anchor of the Warriors' defense. His ability to guard multiple positions and facilitate the offense makes him a unique player. Green is a three-time NBA All-Star and was named the NBA Defensive Player of the Year in 2017.

Conclusion

This 2016-17 Golden State Warriors team is often considered one of the greatest NBA teams of all time because of their dominant regular season and near-perfect playoff run. Their combination of star power, depth, and coaching made them an unsolvable matchup for any team in the league.
